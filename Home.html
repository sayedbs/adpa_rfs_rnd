It’s time to take control of your AD

Atopic dermatitis (AD), the most common form of eczema, is more than skin and itch symptoms. It’s a chronic inflammatory condition that can have an invisible impact on your day-to-day quality of life. You don’t have to live with AD symptoms, including occasional flares. You can work with your doctor and be part of the decision-making in your treatment plan. Start by understanding how AD impacts you and sharing your results.

Living with atopic dermatitis can impact many aspects of your daily life, including causing emotional distress. Imagine what having a better conversation with a doctor could do for you.

Don’t wait to get the care you need

Did you know that it’s been shown that not treating atopic dermatitis appropriately can result in a more significant burden?

That’s because AD isn’t just a skin condition. Beyond the visible symptoms, eczema can also negatively impact overall health including loss of sleep, decline in emotional health, and a lower quality of life.

Building a partnership with your doctor and creating a plan for managing your eczema can help you achieve fewer symptoms.
